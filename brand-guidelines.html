Clearance & Don'ts

  • Maintain at least 16px clearance around the logo on all sides.
  • Do not stretch, rotate, recolor, or apply drop shadows to the logo.
  • Do not place on low-contrast or busy photographic backgrounds without an overlay.
  • Minimum display size: 120px wide for the full lockup; use the icon alone below that.

Identity

Voice & Tone

Written by a proud parent for family and close friends. Warm, celebratory, personal — never clinical or corporate.

Do
  • Write in first-person from the parent's perspective ("Maya played for grandma…")
  • Celebrate small wins enthusiastically ("She finally nailed that tricky left-hand part!")
  • Use friendly, informal language with correct grammar
  • Keep milestone entries short: 2–4 sentences max
  • Use exclamation points sparingly for genuine moments of joy
  • Link to the Instagram video when mentioning a specific performance
Don't
  • Share Maya's full name, school, location, or any identifying details
  • Use overly formal or educational language ("The student demonstrated proficiency…")
  • Over-hype every entry — save excitement for genuine milestones
  • Write long paragraphs; this is a scrapbook, not a blog essay
  • Add marketing or promotional language
  • Include hashtags, SEO keywords, or tracking snippets

Example Milestone Entries

Good example
"Maya played Für Elise for grandma and grandpa over video call today. She only stumbled once on the repeat and then laughed it off and started again. They cried — in the best way. Watch on Instagram →"
Avoid
"Maya Johnson, age 6, a student at Riverside Elementary in Austin TX, successfully completed a rendition of Für Elise by Ludwig van Beethoven during a FaceTime session with her paternal grandparents on March 10, 2026."

Accessibility Reminders

  • All text on colored backgrounds must meet WCAG AA 4.5:1 contrast ratio (3:1 for large text).
  • All images must include descriptive alt text. Decorative images use alt="".
  • Interactive elements need visible focus rings — use box-shadow: 0 0 0 3px purple-50.
  • Use semantic HTML: <main>, <nav>, <article>, <time datetime="">.
  • Emoji icons must have aria-hidden="true" or a meaningful aria-label.
  • No motion-based interactions beyond subtle fade-in — respect prefers-reduced-motion.
